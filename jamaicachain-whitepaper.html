JamaicaChain: A Decentralized Digital Infrastructure for Jamaica's Economic Future

Abstract

This white paper introduces JamaicaChain, a modified Bitcoin-based blockchain infrastructure designed to accelerate Jamaica's digital transformation and establish the nation as a Caribbean blockchain hub. It includes:

  • Parish-based validation
  • Cultural heritage preservation
  • Seamless integration with Jamaica's CBDC (JAM-DEX)

1. Introduction

1.1 Vision

JamaicaChain aims to create a robust digital infrastructure that powers Jamaica's transition into a leading blockchain economy while preserving its unique cultural identity and fostering financial inclusion.

1.2 Background

The evolution of digital currency and blockchain technology represents a four-decade journey of innovation, from which JamaicaChain draws key foundational principles:

1.2.1 Historical Foundations

Digital Privacy and Authentication (1983)
David Chaum's groundbreaking work on blind signatures established the foundational cryptographic techniques that enable modern blockchain privacy. JamaicaChain's identity management system builds upon these principles, implementing parish-level blind signatures for transaction privacy while maintaining regulatory compliance.

Digital Currency Pioneers (1996)
E-gold demonstrated the first successful implementation of digital currency backed by physical assets, processing billions in transactions during its peak. While E-gold ultimately faced regulatory challenges, it provided valuable lessons that inform JamaicaChain's approach to:

  • Regulatory compliance from day one
  • Integration with traditional financial systems
  • The importance of proper KYC/AML procedures
  • Secure asset backing mechanisms

Decentralized Consensus Innovation (1998)
Nick Szabo's Bit Gold proposed the first decentralized digital currency system using proof-of-work, predating Bitcoin. JamaicaChain adapts several key concepts from Bit Gold:

  • The importance of unforgeable costliness
  • Secure property titles (applied to cultural assets)
  • Decentralized consensus mechanisms (modified for parish validation)

1.2.2 Modern Context

As blockchain technology reshapes the global financial landscape, Jamaica stands at a crucial junction. By building on these historical foundations while introducing parish-based validation and cultural preservation mechanisms, JamaicaChain creates a bridge between:

  • Traditional blockchain principles
  • Modern financial requirements
  • Jamaica's unique cultural heritage
  • Parish-based community structures

2.2 Consensus Mechanism

JamaicaChain implements a Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS) consensus mechanism with parish-based validation, combining the efficiency of DPoS with geographical decentralization.

2.2.1 DPoS Implementation

  • Block Producers: 42 active block producers (3 from each of the 14 parishes)
  • Block Time: 3 seconds
  • Round Duration: 126 seconds (42 producers Ã— 3 seconds)
  • Stake Requirement: Minimum 100,000 JMC to qualify as a block producer
  • Voting Power: Proportional to staked amount and staking duration

2.2.2 Parish-Based Producer Selection

Each parish maintains its own pool of block producer candidates with the following rules:

  • Minimum of 3 active producers per parish
  • Maximum of 5 standby producers per parish
  • Producer rotation every 24 hours to ensure fair participation
  • Performance-based reputation system

4. Economic Model

4.1 Token Economics

  • Native token: JMC (Jamaica Coin)
  • Total supply: 1 billion JMC
  • Initial distribution:
    • 40% - Public sale

    • 15% - Ecosystem development
